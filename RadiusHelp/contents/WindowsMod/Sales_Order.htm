Sales Orders

Use the Sales Orders page to record customer’s purchase order details, agreed selling prices, extra charges and shipping details. 

A sales order is an order from a customer to make or supply one or more items. It does not require the existence of an estimate before it can be entered and since items may be supplied from inventory or purchased a sales order does not automatically require a production job.

  1. The sales order is treated as a requirement for the items ordered. The instructions to deliver the goods are entered as shipping requests. Each request is for a single shipment and specifies what is to be shipped, when and to whom.

  2. If there is a production estimate, a production job can be automatically generated from the sales order entry; otherwise the sales order items are processed through the requirements procedures.

  3. Sales order numbers can be automatically generated or manually entered. EFI recommends using automatic numbering. To do this, select the Auto sales order numbering check box on the General Options tab of the Plant Level Options window.

Shipping

Use this section to generate and view shipping instructions. You can also do this on the Shipping Requests window. Shipping requests are a record of what the customer would like shipping on what date and to which location. Shipping requests tend to change as a result of delays in production and alterations by the customer. The shipping request is a key record in EFI Radius because it is used to monitor whether inventory will be produced in time, it forecasts likely sales turnover for a period and it allows the planning of shipments to customers.

Radius uses a header - lines relationship for its shipping requests, meaning that you can combine multiple sales orders onto a single shipping request and shipping note. This is useful in the situation where the customer calls-off different finished product items from several orders that are all to be shipped on the same day. Each request is for one address on one date.

Additionally, you can attach shipping lines to a header that were generated from different sales orders and plants. This is due to Radius using a header - lines relationship for shipping requests. The header contains details of a shipment, such as the customer and the address, while the lines contain everything you want to send with that shipment.

Extra

Use this section to maintain any extra charges associated with the order or to enter a requirement for a credit. This allows extras to be maintained for the sales order without having to open the Billing Requirements window.

An extra charge is an additional cost, not included in the selling price of the finished product, which is to be recharged to the customer. The extra charge may be for pre-press costs, die cutter costs or freight cost, etc. An extra charge must be linked to a sales order.

If extra charges have been defined in the production estimate used to create the sales order then extra charges will be automatically created and appear in the Extras tab. (See Estimating > Estimate Entry/Amend > Pricing > Additional Charges). Any other extra charges can be added manually on this window or on the Job Planning > Billing > Billing Requirements window.

Invoice/Credit Type

Select from the following: 

  1. Invoice with Shipment - The extra charge will not be converted to an invoice until a shipment has been completed for the same sales order or sales order line. This is usually appropriate for freight charges.

  2. Invoice Independently - The extra charge will be converted to an invoice independently of whether a shipment exists or not but the extra may be included on an invoice for a shipment if it can be.

  3. Invoice Separately - The extra charge will be on a separate invoice or credit and not combined with any shipments or other extra charges. T his is usually appropriate for die cutter charges.

  4. Invoice with Other Extras - The extra charge will be combined with any other extra charges but not combined with any shipments. This is usually appropriate for pre press charges.

  5. Credit with Return: The credit extra charge will not be  converted to a credit until finished products relating to the sales order or sales order line have been returned from the customer. This is usually appropriate for freight costs incurred by the customer to return the products.

Required Quantity

Currency Code

The currency this extra will be charged in. This will default to the customer’s currency.

If the Status field under GL Interface is set to XLink Only on the General Ledger tab of the Company > Options > Company Level Options window, you can change the currency to be different to the currency of the customer. In this case, the currency in the Currency code field will initially default from the sales order currency. You can then change this currency if needed. 

 

Tax Code

This may be automatically populated when the Bill Code is selected. 

 

Customer Reference

 Optionally enter the customer purchase order reference if the customer requires a separate purchase order reference for the extra charge.

 

Customer PO Line

Optionally enter the customer’s purchase order line relating to the extra charge. Some customer’s require this information to allow them to record the extra charge in their system.

 

Customer PO Release

Optionally enter the customer's release reference fro the extra charge.

 

Auto Invoice (check box)

Select to automatically create invoices when a shipment is completed. This will automatically default to selected if the Auto invoice sales orders check box is selected on the General Options tab of the Plant > Options > Plant Level Options window. 

When a billing requirement is automatically created, it will default this selection to the billing requirement and the billing requirement will not be set as On Hold. 

Note: For the automatic invoicing to run, you need to set up a Windows Scheduled task.

 

Requirement on Hold (check box)

Select to place the billing requirement intially on hold until it has been approved.
