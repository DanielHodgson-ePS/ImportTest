Sales Orders

Use the Sales Orders page to record customer’s purchase order details, agreed selling prices, extra charges and shipping details. 

A sales order is an order from a customer to make or supply one or more items. It does not require the existence of an estimate before it can be entered and since items may be supplied from inventory or purchased a sales order does not automatically require a production job.

  1. The sales order is treated as a requirement for the items ordered. The instructions to deliver the goods are entered as shipping requests. Each request is for a single shipment and specifies what is to be shipped, when and to whom.

  2. If there is a production estimate, a production job can be automatically generated from the sales order entry; otherwise the sales order items are processed through the requirements procedures.

  3. Sales order numbers can be automatically generated or manually entered. EFI recommends using automatic numbering. To do this, select the Auto sales order numbering check box on the General Options tab of the Plant Level Options window.

Payment Mode

This field displays the status of credit card orders:

  1. Payment Request Sent  - Indicates that the billing process has been run and a payment request has been sent to the integrated product.

  2. Payment Rejected- Indicates that integrated product cannot process the credit card payment. You should contact your customer, and diagnose the issue or arrange payment via a different method.

  3. Payment Received - Indicates that the payment has been sent to your account from your customer's credit card.

  4. Authorization Request Sent - Indicates that your customer's credit card details have been entered into the Wells Fargo Payment Gateway.

  5. Authorization Accepted - Indicates that Wells Fargo has sent confirmation that there is sufficient credit for the payment.

  6. Authorization Declined - Indicates that Wells Fargo has found that the credit is insufficient for the payment.You should contact your customer, and diagnose the issue or arrange payment via a different method.

For more information on the Wells Fargo Payment Gateway Interface, contact EFI support.

Extra

Use this section to maintain any extra charges associated with the order or to enter a requirement for a credit. This section allows extras to be maintained for the sales order without having to open the Billing Requirements window.

An extra charge is an additional cost, not included in the selling price of the finished product, which is to be recharged to the customer. The extra charge may be for pre-press costs, die cutter costs or freight cost, etc. An extra charge must be linked to a sales order.

If extra charges have been defined in the production estimate used to create the sales order then extra charges will be automatically created and appear in the Extras section. See Estimating > Estimate Entry/Amend > Pricing > Additional Charge.

Any other extra charges can be added manually on this window or on the Job Planning > Billing > Billing Requirements window.

Invoice/Credit

Select whether an extra charge is an invoice or a credit from this field. When you select this option, the choices that are available from the Invoice/Credit Type field to the right of this field vary.

 
Invoice/Credit Type

Select from the following: 

  1. Invoice with Shipment - The extra charge will not be converted to an invoice until a shipment has been completed for the same sales order or sales order line. This is usually appropriate for freight charges.

  2. Invoice Independently - The extra charge will be converted to an invoice independently of whether a shipment exists or not but the extra may be included on an invoice for a shipment if it can be.

  3. Invoice Separately - The extra charge will be on a separate invoice or credit and not combined with any shipments or other extra charges. T his is usually appropriate for die cutter charges.

  4. Invoice with Other Extras - The extra charge will be combined with any other extra charges but not combined with any shipments. This is usually appropriate for pre press charges.

  5. Credit with Return: The credit extra charge will not be  converted to a credit until finished products relating to the sales order or sales order line have been returned from the customer. This is usually appropriate for freight costs incurred by the customer to return the products.
